Save Our Sea (S.O.S) Campaign

A nationwide effort by WWF Malaysia focused on protecting marine biodiversity. The campaign includes:

  • Reducing ocean pollution (plastic, ghost nets)
  • Promoting sustainable fishing practices
  • Safeguarding endangered species (turtles, dugongs, corals)
  • Empowering coastal communities through education
  • Campaign Period: Oct 1 ‚Äì Dec 31
  • Peak Week: Nov 4‚Äì10 (World Tsunami Awareness)
  • Year-Round: Educational and environmental activities with schools and communities

Organizer: WWF Malaysia

Target Groups:

  • Students & youth
  • Coastal communities & fishers
  • General public
  • Government, NGOs, corporations
  • Environmental ambassadors & local influencers
  • Core Regions: Sabah, Sarawak, Terengganu, Johor, Penang
  • Nationwide: Social media & school campaigns
  • On-Ground: Beach clean-ups, coral planting, mobile exhibitions

Malaysia is a marine biodiversity hotspot. However, it faces serious threats:

  • 8 million tons of plastic enter oceans annually
  • Species such as turtles, reef fish, and corals are endangered
  • Overfishing damages fish stocks and marine habitats

Protecting the sea protects our food, culture, and future.
